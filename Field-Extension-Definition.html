Section 1.2 Dimension of a field extension

We now state a special property of ring homomorphism from a field.

Lemma 1.2.1.
Let \(F\) be a field and let \(A\) be a nonzero ring with unity. Any ring homomorphism \(f\colon F\to A\) is injective.
Proof.
Hint: Note that \(\ker\,f\) is an ideal of \(F\text{.}\)
Remark 1.2.2.
In view of the above LemmaÂ 1.2.1, given a ring homomorphism from a field \(E\) to a field \(F\) is injective. So, in this way, we may assume that \(E\subseteq F\text{.}\)

Let us define field extension

Definition 1.2.3. (Field Extension).
If a field \(F\) is contained in a field \(K\) then we say that \(K/F\) is a field extension or \(K\) is a field extension ofF.
Remark 1.2.4.
If \(K/F\) is a field extension then \(K\) is a vector space over \(F\text{.}\)
Definition 1.2.5. (Dimension of a field extension).
The dimension of a field extension \(K/F\) is defined to be the dimension of \(K\) over \(F\) as a vector space. We denote dimension of \(K/F\) by \(\dim_FK\) or by \([K:F]\text{.}\)

If \([K:F]\) is finite then we say that \([K:F]\) is a finite field extension

Example 1.2.6.
Let \(F\) be a field. We may construct a field extension of \(F\) by the following procedure. Suppose that \(p(X)\) is an irreducible polynomial in \(F[X]\text{.}\) Note that \(F[X]\) is a principle ideal domain and as \(p(X)\) is irreducible,
\begin{equation*} F[X]/\big(p(X)\big) \end{equation*}
is a field. We will see that \(F[X]/\big(p(X)\big)\) is a finite field extension of \(F\text{.}\)
