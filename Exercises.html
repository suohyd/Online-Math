Section 1 Exercises

We give a series of exercises from basic algebraic Geometry. These exercises are taken from various sources (e.g., Fulton's book, Hartshorne, Gathmann's online notes, etc.). Throuout we assume that \(k\) is an algebraically closed field.

  1. Let \(V=\{(t,t^2,t^3)\in\mathbb{A}^3(\mathbb{C}):t\in\mathbb{C}\}\text{.}\) Show that \(V\) is irreducible.
  2. Let \(Y\subset\mathbb{P}^n(k)\) be an algebraic set not contained in the hyperplane given by the equation \(X_0=0\text{,}\) and let \(U_0\) be an affine patch. Show that the function field \(K(Y)\) is isomorphic to the function field of \(U_0\cap Y\text{,}\) \(K\big(U_0\cap Y\big)\text{.}\)
  3. Find the intersection multiplicity of affine plane curves \(F=y^2-x^3\text{,}\) \(G=x^2-y^3\) at the origin.
  4. Compute the intersection multiplicity at the origin via the algorithm descibed in the class for affine plane curves given by \(F=x^2\) and \(G=xy-x\text{.}\) Does this contradicts the algorithm?
  5. Show that if \(V\subset W\subset\mathbb{P}^n(k)\) are varieties, and \(V\) is a hypersurface, then \(W=V\) or \(W=\mathbb{P}^n\text{.}\)
  6. Is \(\mathbb{A}^1(k)\setminus\{0\}\) an affine variety? Is \(\mathbb{A}^2(k)\setminus\{(0,0)\}\) an affine variety?
  7. Let \(F\) be an irreducible homogeous polynomial in \(k[X_0,X_1,X_2]\) variables. Show that \(V_p(F)\) is infinite, \(\mathbb{P}^2(k)\setminus V_p(F)\) is infinite, and \(V_p(F,G)\) is finite if \(F,G\) have no common components.
  8. Show that \(\mathbb{P}^n(k)\) is a noetherian topological space.
  9. Show that the composition of morphism is a morphism.
  10. If \(Y\) is an open or a closed subvariety of \(X\text{,}\) then the inclusion \(Y\rightarrow X\) is a morphism.
  11. Let \(V\) be an affine variety, and let \(0\neq f\in\Gamma(V)\text{.}\) Let \(V_f=\{P\in V:f(P)\neq 0\}\text{.}\) Show that \(\Gamma(V_f)=\{x/f^n\in k(V):x\in\Gamma(V)\}\text{,}\) and \(V_f\) is an affine variety.
  12. Let \(d\in\mathbb{N}\) and \(N={2+d\choose 2}-1\text{.}\) Suppose that \(M_0,M_1,\ldots,M_N\) are all monomials of degree \(d\) in \(3\) variables. Consider the map \(\rho_d\colon\mathbb{P}^2\to\mathbb{P}^N\) given by \(P=[a_0,a_1,a_2]\mapsto\big[M_0(P),\ldots,M_N(P)\big]\text{.}\) Show that the image of this map is a variety.
  13. Can affine space \(\mathbb{A}^2(k)\) is isomorphic to some projective space \(\mathbb{P}^n(k)\) for some \(n\text{.}\)
  14. Let \(X\) be a variety (affine or quasi-affine or projective or quasi-projective) over \(k\text{,}\) and let \(U\) be a nonempty open subset of \(X\text{.}\) Suppose \(z\in\Gamma(U)\text{,}\) and \(z(P)=0\) for all \(P\in U\text{.}\) Show that \(z=0\text{.}\)
  15. Consider the map \(\varphi\colon V(y-1)\to V(y^3-x^2)\) given by \((x,1)\mapsto(x^3,x^2)\text{.}\) Show that \(\varphi\) is a bijective morphism but the set-theoretic inverse is not a morphism.
