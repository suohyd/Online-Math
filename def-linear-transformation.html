Definition 4.1.1. (Linear Transformation).
Let \(V\) and \(W\) be vector spaces over a field \(F\text{.}\) A mapping \(T\colon V\to W\) is said to be a linear transformation or an \(F\)-linear transformation if for every \(\alpha\in F\) and any \(v,v_1,v_2\in V\) we have

  1. \(\displaystyle T(\alpha v)=\alpha T(v)\)

  2. \(T(v_1+v_2)=T(v_1)+T(v_2)\text{.}\)
