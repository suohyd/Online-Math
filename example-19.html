Example 3.6.4. (A basis for polynomials of degree at most \(n\)).
Let \(F\) be a field and let \(\mathcal{P}_n(F)\) be the subspace of all polynomials in one variable over \(F\) of degree at most \(n\text{.}\) Consider \(\mathfrak{B}=\{1,x,x^2,\ldots,x^{n-1}\}\text{.}\) Check that \(\mathfrak{B}\) is a basis of \(\mathcal{P}_n(F)\text{.}\)

In particular \(\dim_F\big(\mathcal{P}_n(F)\big)=n\text{.}\)
