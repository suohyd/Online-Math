Definition 4.1.1. (Linear Transformation).
Let \(V\) and \(W\) be vector spaces over a field \(F\text{.}\) A mapping \(T\colon V\to W\) is said to be a linear mapping or an \(F\)-linear mapping if

  1. \(T(\alpha v)=\alpha T(v)\quad\text{where }v\in V\text{ and }\alpha\in F\text{.}\)

  2. \(T(v_1+v_2)=T(v_1)+T(v_2)\quad\text{where }v_1,v_2\in V\text{.}\)
