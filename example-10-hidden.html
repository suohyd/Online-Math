Consider an \(n\)-dimensional vector space \(F^n\) over a field \(F\) (refer to ExampleÂ 2.2.2). Let \(e_i=(0,0,\ldots,0,1,0,\ldots,0)\) where \(1\) is at the \(i\)-th place and \(i=1,2,\ldots,n\text{.}\) Consider a subset
\begin{equation*} W=\big\{\alpha_1 e_{i_1}+\alpha_2 e_{i_2}+\cdots\alpha_r e_{i_r}:\alpha_i\in F\text{ and } i_j\in\{1,2,\ldots,n\} \big\}. \end{equation*}
This is a subspace of \(F^n\text{.}\)
