Since \(\mathfrak{B}_V\) is a basis, for a given \(v\in V\) there are uniquely determined \(\alpha_i\in F\) such that \(v=\sum\alpha_iv_i\) (see ExerciseÂ 3.5.11). Then,

\begin{equation*} T(v)=T\big(\sum\alpha_iv_i\big)=\sum\alpha_iT(v_i). \end{equation*}

Hence, \(T\) is completely determined by \(T(v_i)\) for \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\)
