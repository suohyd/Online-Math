Section 1.1 Algebraic Extensions

In this section define algebraic extension and give several examples.

Let us first recall some examples of fields.

  1. The field of rational numbers, \(\mathbb{Q}\text{.}\)
  2. For a prime \(p\text{,}\) \(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}\) is a field. We denote it by \(\mathbb{F}_p.\)
  3. The field of real numbers, \(\mathbb{R}\text{.}\)
  4. The field of complex numbers, \(\mathbb{C}\text{.}\)
  5. The field of rational functions over a field \(F\text{,}\) denoted by \(F(X)\text{.}\) A typical element of \(F(X)\) is of the form
    \begin{equation*} g/h, \end{equation*}
    where \(g,h\) is a polynomials and \(h\) is a nonzero polynomial.
  6. The field of Laurent series field in one variable over \(F\text{,}\) denoted by \(F((X))\text{.}\) A typical element of this field is of the form
    \begin{equation*} \sum_{i\geq n_0}a_iX^i \end{equation*}
    where \(a_i\in F\text{ and }n_0\in\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\)
