Let \(F\) be a field. Let \(e\colon M_{n\times n}(F)\to M_{n\times n}(F)\) be an elementary row operation. Let \(I_n\) be the identity matrix. If \(e\) is the elementary row operation of the first type, i.e., multiplying the \(r\)-th row of \(I_n\) by a nonzero \(\alpha\in F\) then,

\begin{equation*} e(I_n)={\rm diag}(1,1,\ldots,1,\alpha,1,1,\ldots,1). \end{equation*}

Given any \(n\times n\) matrix \(A\in M_{n\times n}(F)\) we get

\begin{equation*} e(I_n)\cdot A=e(A). \end{equation*}
