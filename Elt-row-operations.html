Chapter 1 Elementary row operations

In this preliminary section we state some results and give some exercises related to elementary row operations.

Let \(F\) be a field. Let \(e\colon M_{n\times n}(F)\to M_{n\times n}(F)\) be an elementary row operation. Let \(I_n\) be the identity matrix. If \(e\) is the elementary row operation of the first type, i.e., multiplying the \(r\)-th row of \(I_n\) by a nonzero \(\alpha\in F\) then,

\begin{equation*} e(I_n)={\rm diag}(1,1,\ldots,1,\alpha,1,1,\ldots,1). \end{equation*}

Given any \(n\times n\) matrix \(A\in M_{n\times n}(F)\) we get

\begin{equation*} e(I_n)\cdot A=e(A). \end{equation*}

We also discussed row reduced echelon form of a matrix in the class. Please refere class notes for more details.

Exercises Exercises

Let \(e\colon M_{n\times n}(F)\to M_{n\times n}(F)\) be an elementary row operation (of any type).

1.
Let \(A\in M_{n\times n}(F)\text{.}\) Show that
\begin{equation*} e(I_n)\cdot A=e(A). \end{equation*}
2.
Keep the notations of the above exercise. If we multiply \(A\) by \(e(I_n)\) on the right, i.e., if we consider the matrix \(A\cdot e(I_n)\) then, what matrix one gets? Relate this with "elementary column operations".
3.
Keep notations of the above exercise. Compute determinant of \(e(I_n)\) for each type of elementary row operations. Is \(e(I_n)\) invertible matrix? Justify your answer.
4.
Is "row-equivalence" an equivalence relation?
5.
Let \(A,B\in M_{m\times n}(F)\) be matrices over a field \(F\text{.}\) Assume that homogeneous systems of linear equations \(AX=0\) and \(BX=0\) have same solutions. Is it true that \(A\) and \(B\) are row-equivalent? [Note that this was a question asked by one of the student.]
