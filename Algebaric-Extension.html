Section 1.3 Algebraic Extension

We are now in a position to define algebraic extensions.

Definition 1.3.1.
Let \(K/F\) be a field extension. An element \(\alpha\in K\) is said to be 'algebraic over \(F\)' if \(\alpha\) is a root of a nonzero polynomial with coefficients in \(F\text{.}\)

If every element of \(K\) is algebraic over \(F\) then we say that \(K/F\) is algebraic.

Section 1.3.2 Examples of algebraic extensions

We now give a couple of important examples of algebraic extensions. First, for a field \(F\text{,}\) recall that the ring \(F[X]\) generated by \(F\) and \(X\) is the intersection of all subrings of \(F\) that contain \(F\) and \(X\text{.}\) Furthermore, the field \(F(X)\) generated by \(F\) and \(X\) is the intersection of all subfields of \(F\) that contain \(F\) and \(X\text{.}\)

Example 1.3.1.
Let \(F, K\) be fields and \(\alpha\in K\) be an algebraic element over \(F\text{.}\) Consider the 'evaluation map':
\begin{equation*} {\rm ev_\alpha}:F[X]\to K\quad\text{given by}\quad p(X)\mapsto p(\alpha). \end{equation*}
Note that
  1. \({\rm ev_\alpha}\) is an \(F\)-algebra homomorphism, and image of \({\rm ev_\alpha}\) is a ring \({\rm Im (ev_\alpha)}=\{p(\alpha):p(x)\in F[x]\}\text{.}\)
  2. By closure of addition and multiplication, the image of \({\rm ev_\alpha}\) is contained in every subring that contains \(F\) and \(\alpha\text{.}\) Hence, \(F[\alpha]={\rm Im (ev_\alpha)}\text{.}\)
  3. By the isomorphism theorem,
    \begin{equation*} F[X]/\ker(\rm ev_\alpha)\simeq F[\alpha], \end{equation*}
    a subring of \(K\text{.}\) In particular, \(\ker(\rm ev_\alpha)\) is a prime ideal. Since, \(F[X]\) is a PID, every prime ideal is a maximal ideal, and there exists an irreducible polynomial, say \(m_{\alpha,F}\text{,}\) that generates \(\ker(\rm ev_\alpha)\text{.}\)
  4. Therefore, \(F[\alpha]=F(\alpha)\text{.}\)
Remark 1.3.2.
The polynomial \(m_{\alpha,F}\) in the ExampleÂ 1.3.1 is called the minimal polynomial of \(\alpha\) over \(F\text{.}\) Note that if \(p(X)\in F[X]\) is a polynomial having \(\alpha\) as a root then \(p(X)\in\ker({\rm ev_\alpha})\text{,}\) and hence \(m_{\alpha,F}\) divides \(p(X)\text{.}\)
